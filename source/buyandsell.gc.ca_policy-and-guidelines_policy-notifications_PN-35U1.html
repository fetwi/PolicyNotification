Background

This policy notification provides updated guidance for implementing
the recently approved SOS Delegation Approval Limits.

In December 2001, Supply Operations Service Branch received approval
to implement a new delegation of approval authorities. In March
2002, the new Minister made further changes to the delegations.
This Policy Notification incorporates all of the changesand
is effective as of Monday March 18, 2002.

Supply Manual

1. The tables in Annex 6.1.2 of the Supply Manual, setting out delegations for Goods;
Services; Telecommunications; Construction; and, A&E Services,
are updated and superseded by the five tables at Annex A of this
Policy Notification.

These new tables also supersede the delegations which appear in
the departmental delegation of authorities which appears on the
PWGSC intranet website (accessible to PWGSC employees only).

Note that the non-competitive contract entry approval authority
for goods (Annex 6.1.2 in the Supply Manual), granted to Chiefs is revised to
$150K, to correct a previous error in the Supply Manual.

2. Exceptional Contract Approval Authorities are amended as follows:

  1. (agricultural products for foreign aid programs) Section 6.1.6
    (a) is amended to read as follows:

    (b) if the total amount payable under the agreement, inclusive
    of any amendments, exceeds the limits in Annex 6.1.2 but does
    not exceed$5 million: all positions
    in Level 1    .

  2. (transport by ocean-going vessel for CIDA) Section 6.1.7 (b)
    is amended to read as follows:

    (b) if the aggregate payable under the agreement, including
    any amendments thereto, exceeds the limits in Annex 6.1.2 but
    does not exceed $2.5 million: all positions in Level
    1    .

  3. (repair and overhaul of military equipment) Section 6.1.8 (b)
    is amended to read as follows:

    (b) if the contract, including any amendments thereto, exceeds
    the limits in Annex 6.1.2 but does not exceed $25 million:
    all positions in Level 1.

  4. (munitions) Section 6.1.11 (b) is amended to read as follows:

    (b) if the total payable under the contract, inclusive of
    any amendments, exceeds the limits of Annex 6.1.2 but does
    not exceed $25 million: all positions in Level 1.

  5. (Foreign Military Sales) Section 6.1.12 (b) is amended to read
    as follows:

    (b) if the total amount payable under the contract, inclusive
    of any amendments, exceeds the limits in Annex 6.1.2 but does
    not exceed$12.5 million: all positions in Level 1.

  6. (advertising) Section 6.1.14 (b) is amended to read as follows:

    (b) if the total amount payable under the contract, inclusive
    of any amendments, exceeds the limits in Annex 6.1.2 but does
    not exceed $10 million: all positions in Level 1.

3. Procurement Information Form (PIF)

The following changes to the PIF process are now applicable. All
other aspects of the PIF process, as referenced in Annex 6.1.8 of the Supply Manual remain active and applicable.

PIFs are required for Services; Telecommunications; Construction;
and A&E contracts, at the same value thresholds Supply has
used for the past several years.

PIFs are not required for any Goods contracts.

PIFs are not required for any amendments.

PIFs are required for each covered contract: at the Planning stage
(24 hours before a notice of procurement is posted); and, at Contract
Award (24 hours before the Award).

The chart below supersedes the previous chart in the Supply
Manual, Annex 6.1.8

4. Conditions Imposed

Effective immediately, Annex 6.1 (1) (b) and (c) of the Supply Manual are modified,
by removing from the lists of position titles "Regional Directors/Directors,
European and Washington Directorates".

5. Conditional Emergency Contracting Authority

An increase in approval authority from Treasury Board for Emergency
Contracting to $15M has been approved. This authority rests with
the Minister.

Conditional Emergency Contracting Authority

Authority limits apply to the cumulative value of the contract
including amendments

Departmental Limit
$15M
Level 1
Level 2
Level 3
Level 4

This emergency contracting authority can be used only if all of
the following criteria are met:

  • the action is in response to pressing emergencies where there
    will be significant human and/or financial risks;
  • the Minister invokes the National Security or Extreme Urgency
    provisions of the applicable trade agreements;
  • the requirement cannot be satisfied by normal contracting procedures
    due to the urgency of the situation;
  • the applicable departmental Minister approves the use off these
    special authorities;
  • and the new exceptional contracting authorities
    remain subject to the reporting requirements for the use of emergency
    contracting as set out in the TB Contracting Policy.

6. Certification of Defence Supplies

Section 6.1.21 is amended to read as follows:

6.1.21 Certifications required by the Canada Customs and Revenue
Agency from PWGSC that items imported by the Department of National
Defence are "defence supplies", as defined in the Defence Production
Act, pursuant to Order-in-Council P.C. 1966-2184, as amended P.C.
1967-969, and section 17 of the Financial Administration Act:

Directors General, Supply Operations and Regions; Senior
Directors and Directors, headquarters; and, Regional Directors.

7. Table of Equivalent Positions

The Table of Equivalent Positions which appears in Annex 6.1.2
is amended, by adding:

  • to level 3, Supply Team Leader, Supply Specialist, Senior Engineering
    Procurement Officer, Contract Management Officer (MCP); and,
  • to Level 4, Supply Officer.
