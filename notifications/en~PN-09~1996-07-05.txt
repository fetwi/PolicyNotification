<p>Distribution, to senior management, of CPAAs, procurement plans and contract summaries for requirements over $1 million is a condition of the Minister's delegation of contract approval authorities.</p>
<p>Further to the standard distribution prescribed by the <em>Supply Manual</em> (procedures 6.656 and 6.667) and complementary internal procedures, CPAAs, procurement plans and contract summaries for procurements valued greater than $1 million must be provided to the following distribution lists:</p>
<h3>Procurement Plans and Contract Summaries:</h3>
<p>Alan Williams, (ADM, SOSB)<br/>
					Mary-Lou Devine, (ADM, SOSB)<br/>
					Catherine McCarthy, (ADM, SOSB)<br/>
					Ellen Beall, (Sr. General Counsel)<br/>
					Darlene Kulka, (Legal)<br/>
					Steven Turner, (Audit and Review)<br/>
					Gian Brown, (Audit and Review)<br/>
					Sector/Regional Director General</p>
<h3>CPAAs:</h3>
<p>Alan Williams, (ADM, SOSB)<br/>
					Mary-Lou Devine, (ADM, SOSB)<br/>
					Catherine McCarthy, (ADM, SOSB)<br/>
					Sector/Regional Legal Counsel<br/>
					Sector/Regional Director General<br/>
					Steven Turner, (Audit and Review)<br/>
					Gian Brown, (Audit and Review)</p>
<p>Additional recipients (Director, Sector/Regional CQC) may be added<br/>
					as appropriate.</p>
<p>A listing of Legal Counsel by Product Line or Division is attached.</p>
<p>The Minister's condition on delegation related to contracts valued<br/>
					greater than $1 million includes the requirement to delay <strong>contract<br/>
					awards</strong> for a period of 48 hours after distribution of<br/>
					notices to senior management.</p>
<p>This is intended to provide the list<br/>
					of mandatory recipients of CPAAs and contract summaries a reasonable<br/>
					opportunity to view the approval document, and provide comments<br/>
					if deemed appropriate prior to entry into contract.</p>
<p>The condition to delay procurement actions for 48 hours is not<br/>
					related to the procurement planning activity. Therefore, solicitation<br/>
					activities may proceed as soon as the procurement plan or the CPAA<br/>
					is approved.</p>
<p>CPAAs must be resubmitted where there has been a significant change<br/>
					from the originally approved plan.</p>
