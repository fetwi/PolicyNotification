<h2 id="PN-87no1">Introduction</h2>
<p>The purpose of Policy Notification (PN) 87 is to revise the common service acquisition authorities, associated conditions and related procedures in the Supply Manual. On April 17, 2009, the Minister approved a new departmental Delegation of Authority instrument. Schedule 3 of this instrument sets out the maximum limits for common service acquisition authorities. The Supply Manual tables of approval and signing authority limits have been modified to reflect changes as a result of the new delegation instrument.</p>
<h2 id="PN-87no2">Policy</h2>
<p>Effective immediately, contract approval and signing authority limits, and associated conditions and related procedures, are revised. The delegation limit for goods and services for the Assistant Deputy Minister (ADM), Acquisition Branch, has increased from 75 to 100 percent of the Minister's authorities as per Schedule 3. A summary of the revisions to the Supply Manual is provided below. </p>
<p>Although the new contract approval and signing authority limits are effective immediately, the authorities for individual positions will only be effective when they are recognized on new individual delegation forms. All contracting staff must complete a new Delegation of Common Service Acquisition Authorities - Schedule 3 form. The revised contract approval and signing authority limits cannot be exercised until the form is completed. The revised limits are valid for new procurements and for those already in progress. More information on completing the new delegation form can be found in Section II below.</p>
<p>This <acronym title="Policy Notification">PN</acronym> also serves to notify contracting staff of the deletion from the Supply Manual of: references to the Electronic Data Processing Buy for Lease Program; Annex 6.1.9: Contract Amendment Approval Process (CAAP); and Annex 6.1.10: Just in Time Approval Process (JITAP).</p>

<h2 id="PN-87no4">Section II. Delegation Form</h2>
<p>The individual delegation form is used to specify the limits that are set by management for the incumbent of each position. The authority granted to an incumbent of a position must be commensurate with the level required to carry out the responsibilities assigned to the incumbent. This relationship between responsibilities and authorities for each incumbent is recognized on individual delegation forms.</p>
<h3>Procedures to update the individual delegation form </h3>
<p>Superiors must ensure that: </p>
<ol class="double-space" type="a">
<li>a new individual delegation form (<span lang="fr"><acronym title="Public Works and Government Services Canada - Travaux publics et Services gouvernementaux Canada">PWGSC-TPSGC</acronym></span> 524 in <acronym title="Electronic Forms">ELF</acronym>), is completed for each incumbent to reflect the revised authorities;</li>
<li>the signed original individual delegation form is forwarded to the office of the Director General, Policy, Risk, Integrity and Strategic Management Sector, Acquisition Policy and Process Directorate, Attention: Susan Blakeney (Regional offices are requested to send a copy of the signed form); and</li>
<li>a signed copy is kept on file in the procurement area or central location.</li>
</ol>
<p>General information on Delegation of Authority Instruments is available to <acronym title="Public Works and Government Services Canada">PWGSC</acronym> employees on-line at <a href="http://source.pwgsc-tpsgc.gc.ca/gos/delegation/menu_e.html" rel="external">Delegation of Authorities</a> <span> (accessible only on the Government of Canada network)</span>. Further information on the principles of delegation of authority and proper use of the authorities is available at <a href="http://source.pwgsc-tpsgc.gc.ca/gos/delegation/chap3_e.html" rel="external">Delegation Principles</a> <span> (accessible only on the Government of Canada network)</span>.</p>
<p>Contracting officers must ensure that any authorities they exercise are in accordance with their approved delegation form.</p>

<p><strong>Legend:</strong></p>
<p>C = Contract Signing Authority<br/>
					  A = Aggregate Contract Amendment Signing Authority</p>
<p><strong>Position Titles:</strong>  Position titles shown above are examples only - all equivalent positions (as shown in the Table of Equivalent Positions following the approval authority charts) have the same authorities as indicated above.</p>
<p><strong>Ratifications:</strong>  Authorities for approving agreements which involve: pre-contractual work, ratification of contractual commitments, confirming orders, or contracts/amendments which include pre-contractual work clauses, or any other retroactive elements are limited to 50 percent of the non-competitive dollar thresholds specified above. The minimum approval authority is the Director level. The 50 percent reduction and the limitation to non-competitive contract approval authority limits apply to all positions below assistant deputy minister level.</p>
<p>The relevant limits for individual officers, which may be lower than the maximums delegated to the levels above, are contingent upon Acquisitions Branch policies and procedures.  Contracting officers must ensure that any authorities they exercise are in accordance with their approved individual delegation form.</p>
<p>The limits shown in this table are expressed in terms of thousands (K) or millions (M) of dollars or, where dollars are not appropriate, in terms of policy or regulatory constraints.  The word "Full" means that the authority limit is subject to the lesser of <acronym title="Treasury Board">TB</acronym>/departmental restriction or budgetary limit (i.e. the availability of funds).</p>

<h5>Column 46 Conditional Emergency Contracting Authority</h5>
<p>Approval authorities for non-competitive contracts and amendments, for goods and services up to $15M rests with the Minister of <acronym title="Public Works and Government Services Canada">PWGSC</acronym> and the Assistant Deputy Minister, Acquisitions Branch, in response to pressing emergencies by departments where there will be significant human and/or financial risk on condition that a report be sent to the Treasury Board (TB) Secretariat within 60 calendar days of the authorization or beginning of work.</p>
<p>This emergency contracting authority <strong>can be used only if all</strong> of the following criteria are met:</p>
<ol class="double-space" type="a">
<li>the Assistant Deputy Minister, Acquisitions Branch, invokes the National Security or Extreme Urgency provisions of the applicable trade agreements;</li>
<li>the requirement cannot be satisfied by normal contracting procedures due to the urgency of the situation; and</li>
<li>the applicable client departmental minister requests that <acronym title="Public Works and Government Services Canada">PWGSC</acronym> Minister or Assistant Deputy Minister, Acquisitions Branch, approve the use of this authority.</li>
</ol>
<p>This authority remains subject to the reporting requirement for the use of emergency contracting as set out in the <acronym title="Treasury Board">TB</acronym> Contracting Policy. </p>

<h2>Appendix F</h2>
<h3>Annex 6.1.5: Contract Amendment Approval Instructions<br/>
					(2009-XX-XX)</h3>
<p>When the correct approval authority level for a proposed amendment is being determined, consideration must be given not only to the value of the amendment but also to the cumulative value (or aggregate) of amendments. The amendment approval authority level is based on the aggregate amendment value excluding negative and pre-approved amendments.  Following is an example of how to determine the appropriate authority for amendments to a contract awarded on a competitive basis. </p>
<p><strong>1. Determining the Amendment Approval Authority for Competitive Contracts for Goods</strong></p>
<p>A Contract Planning and Advance Approval (CPAA) was approved at the director level for a total original procurement value estimated at $10,000,000.  The electronic bidding process was utilized. The original contract was awarded at $8,500,000 and signed by the Manager, the applicable signing authority level. The <acronym title="Contract Planning and Advance Approval">CPAA</acronym> included a quantity option equaling $1,000,000 to be exercised at anytime before the expiry date of the contract. The <acronym title="Contract Planning and Advance Approval">CPAA</acronym> also included a set aside for design changes equaling $500K.</p>
<p>The quantity option  and the set aside for design changes were  not funded at the time of contract award and were not included in the contract value. </p>
<p>Amendments 1 to 10 are determined as follows:</p>
<ol class="double-space">
<li>Amendment 1: not pre-approved, is needed to add some items equaling $28,400.
<p>The amendment approval authority is the Supply Specialist.</p>
<p>The Signing Authority is the Intern Officer/Trainee.</p>
<p>Revised contract value is now $8,528,400.</p>
</li>
<li>Amendment 2: not pre-approved, is for a mutually agreeable one month delay  in the estimated delivery date of the first shipment (i.e. delivery date is not firm). A NIL amendment.
<p>Amendment aggregate value for not pre-approved amendments is $28,400.</p>
<p>NIL value amendments where no further risk or liability accrue to Canada are administrative in nature and require no approvals.</p>
<p>The signing authority is the Intern Officer/Trainee.</p>
<p>Revised contract value is now $8,528,400 (no change).</p>
</li>
<li>Amendment 3: a pre-approved option quantity, exercises half of the option quantity amount equaling $500,000.
<p>Aggregate amendment values for not pre-approved amendments are separate from the aggregate amendment values of either options or set asides. If required, options or set asides can be exercised to the maximum value in one amendment.</p>
<p>Cumulative amendments to exercise a pre-approved option is $500,000.</p>
<p>The amendment to exercise an option has already been pre-approved at the <acronym title="Contract Planning and Advance Approval">CPAA</acronym> stage.</p>
<p>The signing authority is the Supply Team Leader.</p>
<p>Revised contract value is now $9,028,400.</p>
</li>
<li>Amendment 4: not pre-approved, is for unscheduled work equaling $76,400.
<p>Amendment aggregate value for not pre-approved amendments is $104,800.</p>
<p>The amendment approval authority is the Manager.</p>
<p>The signing authority is the Supply Specialist.</p>
<p>Revised contract value is now $9,104,800;</p>
</li>
<li>Amendment 5: pre-approved option quantity exercises the second half of the option quantity for a value of $500,000.
<p>The cumulative value for the pre-approved option is $1,000,000. The option is now fully exercised.</p>
<p>The amendment to exercise an option has already been pre-approved at the <acronym title="Contract Planning and Advance Approval">CPAA</acronym> stage.</p>
<p>The signing authority is the Supply Team Leader.</p>
<p>Revised contract value is now $9,604,800.</p>
</li>
<li>Amendment 6: not previously approved, a NIL amendment is required to add a SACC clause, inadvertently omitted from the original document.
<p>Amendment aggregate value for not pre-approved amendments is $104,800.</p>
<p>NIL value amendments where no further risk or liability accrue to Canada are administrative in nature and require no approvals.</p>
<p>The signing authority is the Supply Specialist.</p>
<p>Revised contract value is now: $9,604,800. No change.</p>
</li>
<li>Amendment 7: pre-approved set aside, is raised to exercise a portion of the pre-approved set aside for design changes equaling $280,000.
<p>The cumulative value for amendments to exercise the pre-approved set aside is $280,000.</p>
<p>The amendment approval authority is the Manager.</p>
<p>The signing authority is the Supply Team Leader.</p>
<p>Amendments issued to use an amount set aside for unscheduled work, work arisings, or design changes must be approved by the appropriate non-competitive contract entry approval authority, not to exceed the Director level.</p>
<p>Aggregate amendment values of set asides, previously approved, are separate from the aggregate amendment values of either options or normal amendments. If required, options or set asides can be exercised to the maximum value in one amendment.</p>
<p>Revised contract value is now $9,884,800;</p>
</li>
<li>Amendment 8: pre-approved set aside, is raised to exercise a portion of the pre-approved set aside for design changes equaling $100,000.
<p>The cumulative value for amendments to exercise the pre-approved set aside is $380,000. Balance remaining is $120,000.</p>
<p>* The set aside amendment value stands alone: non-competitive contract entry approval authority is sought for $100,000.</p>
<p>The amendment approval authority is the Supply Specialist.</p>
<p>The signing authority is the Supply Officer.</p>
<p>Revised contract value is now $9,984,800.</p>
</li>
<li>Amendment 9: not pre-approved, is for unscheduled work equaling $210,000.
<p>Amendment aggregate for not pre-approved amendments is now $314,800.</p>
<p>As per the Electronic Bidding Aggregate Amendment levels, the Director can approve amendments to a maximum aggregate amendment value of $500,000.</p>
<p>The amendment approval authority is the Director.</p>
<p>The signing authority is the Supply Team Leader based on aggregate amendment amount.</p>
<p>Revised contract value is now $10,194,800.</p>
</li>
<li>Amendment 10: not pre-approved, the client decides, due to cut backs, that Amendment 9 is no longer required. The supplier agrees to forego this work at no additional cost to Canada, (having just recently ordered materials and was able to negotiate no cancellation fees, etc. from his suppliers) for a negative value amendment of $210,000.
<p>Amendment aggregate value for not pre-approved amendments remains at $314,800.</p>
<p>The amendment approval authority is the Contracting Authority, designated as such in the contract.</p>
<p>The signing authority is the Supply Team Leader based on aggregate amendment amount.</p>
<p>Revised contract value is now $9,984,800.</p>
</li>

<p><strong>2. Reductions in Contract Value</strong></p>
<ol class="double-space" type="a">
<li><strong>Situation:</strong> The client reduces the quantity required and the supplier/contractor agrees to the reduced quantity with no increase in the unit price.
<p><strong>Approval Level:</strong> The contracting authority, designated as such in the contract, may approve the amendment that reduces the value of the contract (if amendment is deemed administrative in nature).</p>
</li>
<li><strong>Situation:</strong> The client reduces the quantity required but the supplier/contractor wants to increase the price because of the reduced quantity. Renegotiation is necessary.
<p><strong>Approval Level:</strong> The approval level for the contract amendment is that required for the revised unit price multiplied by the new quantity. Aggregate amendment approval authorities apply.</p>
</li>
<li><strong>Situation:</strong> The client wishes to disencumber funds allocated to a repair and overhaul contract  in the last quarter of the fiscal year, because no more work arising will be forthcoming during that period.
<p><strong>Approval Level:</strong> The contracting authority, designated as such in the contract, may approve an amendment to reduce the value of the contract.</p>
</li>
<li><strong>Situation:</strong> The client requires a work package to be removed from a research and development contract. There is no clear relationship between the reduced cost and the reduced work package.
<p><strong>Approval Level:</strong> The amendment will be approved at a level equal to the value of the proposed cost reduction. Aggregate amendment approval authorities apply. Nil value amendment is not appropriate as there is no link between the value of the work package and the reduction.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>3. Substitute Item (or work package)</strong></p>
<p>If a client requests the contracting authority to amend a contract by deleting an item (or work package) and substituting a different item (or work package), the value of the substitute item (or work package) will determine the contract amendment approval level. If the contract amendment approval level, based on the value of the substitute item (or work package) exceeds the Director General's approval authority, the standard method of determining the amendment approval authority, based on cumulative value of amendments, must be used to determine whether the Deputy Minister, the Minister, or <acronym title="Treasury Board">TB</acronym> approval is required.</p>

<p><strong>4. Additional Risks</strong></p>
<p>Contract amendments that propose changes to either the basis of payment, the method of payment or the contract conditions, so that additional risk or liability is transferred to Canada, must be authorized at or above the original approval level for that contract only if corresponding compensatory benefits accrue to Canada. In the absence of corresponding benefits, such changes would constitute extra payments for which only <acronym title="Treasury Board">TB</acronym> has approval authority.</p>
<p>The following are examples of changes that would constitute additional risk to Canada:</p>
<ol type="a">
<li>liberalizing the progress payments;</li>
<li>eliminating the requirement for a performance bond;</li>
<li>slippage, by the contractor, of firm delivery dates.</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>5. Advance Approval for Amount Set Aside</strong></p>
<ol class="double-space" type="a">
<li>If the original authority to enter into a contract also included advance approval for an amount to be set aside for unscheduled work, design changes or work arisings, then on each occasion when any of the amount set aside is used, it must be approved by the appropriate non-competitive contract entry approval authority, not to exceed the Director level.</li>
<li>If an amendment to use the remainder of the amount set aside for a specific purpose exceeds that amount, the approval authority for the excess amount will revert back to the appropriate aggregate amendment approval authority in accordance with Annex 6.1.2. Aggregate amendment values of set asides, previously approved, are separate from the aggregate amendment values of either options or normal amendments. If required, set asides can be exercised to the maximum value in one amendment.</li>
<li>If a condition of the original approval requires other levels of approval for encumbering amounts set aside, the levels established in the original contract approval will take precedence.</li>
<li>The amount of the set aside/option should be based on sound front-end planning and preparation. The better the planning and preparation the better the ability to predict additional quantities or unforeseen work arisings, and the more accurate the assigned dollars values. A procurement plan that is thoroughly justified as to the purpose of each planned expenditure is more likely to be approved.</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>6. Advance Approval for Options</strong></p>
<p>Once approval has been obtained to exercise an option, the contract amendment to exercise the option requires only the contract amendment signing authority as per Annex 6.1.2. Funds and approval received for an option can only be used for the specific purpose stated in the approval document. Any change to the scope or period of the option must be approved by the original approval authority.</p>
<p>Aggregate amendment values of options, previously approved, are separate from the aggregate amendment values of either set asides or normal amendments. If required, options can be exercised to the maximum value in one amendment.</p>
<p>Approval and funding for unexpected changes in the scope of the work, that were not specifically provided for in the approval document as an Advance Approval for Amount Set Aside (see section 5 above), cannot be drawn from the approval for an option. For example, if approval is obtained for a 1 year services contract valued at $200,000, with an option year also valued at $200,000, the contracting officer cannot use the approval and funding related to the option year to cover an increase in the services required during the first year. Any change in the scope of work that was not approved as part of the original submission is a change in the requirement and must be approved on its own merits.</p>

<p><strong>7. Contract Code for Pre-Approved Amendments</strong></p>
<p>Document Type number 22, Contract Code for Pre-Approved Amendments, is available in ABE (Automated Buyer Environment) and SELECT systems, and is used to identify all pre-approved amendments.</p>
<p>These include, but are not limited to:</p>
<ol class="double-space" type="a">
<li>amendments issued to exercise an option for additional quantities or years that were included in the original bid solicitation and approved as part of the original contract approval submission; and,</li>
<li>amendments which use amounts set-aside for anticipated, but not yet clearly defined, changes (e.g. unscheduled work, design changes, work arisings), which were approved as part of the original contract approval submission.</li>
</ol>
<p>Amendments that do not fit within the description of the original approval or go beyond the scope or value of the approval will be coded as "Normal Amendments" and must be approved at the appropriate level as determined by the Minister's delegation of amendment authorities.</p>
<p><strong>8. Rates (or Prices-to-be-negotiated)/Interim Rates (or Prices)</strong></p>
<p>If the basis of payment in a contract includes interim rates (or prices) and it is proposed to amend the contract to provide for firm rates (or prices) in lieu, then the amendment approval authority level will be as follows (unless otherwise instructed by the contract approval authority):</p>
<ol class="double-space" type="a">
<li>one level above that required for signing the contract, only if the firm rates (or prices) are equal to or less than the interim rates (or prices); or</li>
<li>at the original contract approval authority level, when the firm rates (or prices) exceed the interim rates (or prices); or</li>
<li>Director General, if the contract was approved by the Assistant Deputy Minister, the Minister or <acronym title="Treasury Board">TB</acronym>.</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>9. Combining Several Types of Contract Changes in One Amendment</strong></p>
<p>When a contract amendment is raised to incorporate several types of changes enumerated in paragraphs 1 to 7 above (i.e. firming up price-to-be-negotiated items, using a portion of an amount set aside, addition of items/quantities, etc.), the highest approval level for any one type of these changes or combinations thereof being incorporated into the contract by that amendment must apply.</p>
<p><strong>10. Amendments to Contracts with Former Public Servants</strong></p>
<p>All amendments to service contracts with former public servants in receipt of a pension pursuant to the <em>Public Service Superannuation Act</em> must be approved by <acronym title="Treasury Board">TB</acronym> when the total amount payable exceeds the thresholds set out in procedure 6A.006. </p>
<p><strong>11. Interpretation of Aggregate Amendment Value</strong></p>
<p>All amendment approval authorities (for amendments not pre-approved) are to be determined on the basis of the aggregate amendment value.</p>
<p>The interpretation that aggregate amendment value means the sum total of the amendments to a contract including positive and negative amendments applies only to amendments requiring <acronym title="Treasury Board">TB</acronym> approval. For internal purposes and to ensure adequate control measures are in place, the aggregate amount for amendments not pre-approved will be calculated using only positive amendments.</p>
<p>As a general principle, once an approval level has been reached it can never be lowered. As an example, once the ministerial level for approval has been reached every subsequent positive amendment reverts to the minister regardless of dollar value.</p>
<p>Nil value amendments where no further risk or liability accrue to Canada are administrative in nature and can be approved and signed by the Contracting Authority.  Nil value amendments where further risk or liability will be Canada's responsibility require approval authority at or above the original approval authority.</p>
<p><strong>12. Amendment Approved by Treasury Board</strong></p>
<p>When <acronym title="Treasury Board">TB</acronym> has approved an amendment, <acronym title="Public Works and Government Services Canada">PWGSC</acronym> may further amend the contract without <acronym title="Treasury Board">TB</acronym> approval if the cumulative value of the amendments after each issuance of an amendment pursuant to a <acronym title="Treasury Board">TB</acronym> approval does not exceed the non-competitive contract amendment approval authority limits set out in 6A.020.</p>
